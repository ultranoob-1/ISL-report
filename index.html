2.0 // Module I: UAV Systems & Control

A deep dive into Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), covering the full spectrum from physical hardware integration to the software logic for autonomous operation.

Hardware Integration Lab

The module began with the hands-on assembly of a quadcopter drone. This exercise was crucial for understanding the physical constraints and interdependencies of a UAV's core components.

  • Structural Assembly: Constructed the drone's airframe, focusing on achieving maximum rigidity while minimizing mass. Mounted and balanced the brushless motors to mitigate vibrations.
  • Avionics Integration: Methodically wired the Power Distribution Board, ESCs, and Flight Controller, emphasizing robust connections and signal integrity to prevent system failure.
  • Firmware & Calibration: Utilized mission planning software to flash the flight controller firmware and perform essential sensor calibrations (IMU, magnetometer), ensuring accurate flight data.

Autonomous Control Theory

The focus then shifted from the physical to the logical, exploring the algorithms that enable drones to perceive their environment and navigate intelligently.

  • Sensor Fusion Algorithms: Studied how Kalman filters are used to fuse data from gyroscopes, accelerometers, and GPS to compute a reliable estimate of the drone's state (position and orientation).
  • PID Control Loops: Gained a practical understanding of tuning Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controllers, learning how to optimize flight stability and responsiveness.
  • Path Planning: Explored foundational pathfinding algorithms like A* and RRT, understanding their application in generating collision-free trajectories for autonomous missions.

3.0 // Module II: Pico-Satellite Design Lifecycle

A simulated end-to-end satellite mission, from requirements definition to testing, instilling principles of systems engineering and collaborative design.

Capstone: "Project DRISHTI"

Our capstone project was to design a CanSat capable of capturing a low-resolution image during its descent and transmitting it to a ground station using Slow-Scan Television (SSTV).

Mission Parameters

  • Primary Goal: Image acquisition and transmission. Secondary goal was to log basic atmospheric data (temperature, pressure).
  • My Contribution: Led the development of the payload and communications subsystems. This involved interfacing an OV7670 camera module with the microcontroller and implementing the SSTV encoding library.
  • Major Hurdle: The microcontroller lacked sufficient RAM to buffer a full image frame, making direct SSTV encoding impossible.
  • Innovative Solution: I developed a "line-by-line" processing pipeline. The code would read a single line of pixels from the camera, process and encode it into an audio signal for SSTV, transmit it, and then discard the line from memory before fetching the next one. This minimized RAM usage and made the mission feasible.

4.0 // Module III: Launch Systems & Propulsion

An examination of the fundamental physics and engineering challenges associated with achieving orbital and sub-orbital flight.

Rocketry Principles

  • Ideal Rocket Equation: Deconstructed the Tsiolkovsky rocket equation to understand the critical roles of mass ratio and specific impulse ($I_{sp}$) in determining a launch vehicle's total $\Delta v$.
  • Propulsion Architectures: Compared solid vs. liquid propulsion systems, analyzing their respective advantages in thrust, efficiency, and controllability for different mission phases.
  • Dynamic Stability: Learned the principle of ensuring the Center of Pressure (CP) is located behind the Center of Gravity (CG) to maintain aerodynamic stability during ascent.

Model Rocketry Lab

We applied these principles in a practical workshop, building and launching model rockets. Pre-launch simulations in OpenRocket were used to verify stability and predict performance. The successful launch and recovery provided a tangible validation of the theoretical concepts and safety protocols.

5.0 // Module IV: Geospatial Data Analysis

This module focused on converting raw satellite imagery into actionable intelligence using Geographic Information Systems (GIS).

Case Study: Coastal Erosion Monitoring

We used QGIS and multi-temporal Landsat imagery to analyze changes in the coastline of a region in Odisha over a decade. The goal was to quantify erosion and deposition patterns.

Analysis Workflow

  • Water Body Extraction: Used the Modified Normalized Difference Water Index (MNDWI) to accurately delineate the boundary between land and water in satellite images from two different years.
  • Change Detection: Converted the raster water maps into vector polygons. By overlaying the polygons from the two time periods, we could precisely calculate the area of land lost to erosion and gained through accretion.
  • Key Finding: The analysis revealed a net erosion of several hundred meters in specific high-risk zones, providing critical data for local authorities to plan mitigation strategies. This demonstrated GIS as a powerful tool for environmental monitoring.

6.0 // Module V: The New Space Industry

An analysis of the commercial and entrepreneurial forces reshaping the space sector in India and globally.

Ecosystem Analysis

We examined the strategic shift in India's space policy, with IN-SPACe acting as a facilitator for private industry. The discussion focused on the emerging synergy between ISRO's institutional knowledge and the agile innovation of startups in launch, satellites, and downstream applications.

Career Vectoring

The module explored the diversification of career paths in the space industry, highlighting the growing demand for specialists in data science, AI/ML, robotics, systems engineering, and non-technical roles like venture capital, law, and policy.
